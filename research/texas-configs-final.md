## Config 1: §113.49 — Personal Financial Literacy (HB 2662/2013, HB 27/2023)

**Course type:** Standalone PFL elective (half-credit)
**K&S statements:** 16 — (c)(1) through (c)(16)
**Categories:** Earning/Spending (4), Saving/Investing (3), Credit/Borrowing (3), Insuring/Protecting (4), College/Postsecondary (2)

### Reed's mapping produced 40 chapters. To reach 45, adding 5:

| Addition | Title | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| **L-71** | Introduction to Estate Planning | **Required.** (c)(14) explicitly requires wills, guardianship, POA, living will, medical directives. Load-bearing — no other chapter covers this. |
| **L-42** | Resume Building & Personal Branding | Strengthens (c)(15)/(c)(16) postsecondary/career readiness. L-41 (Career Exploration) is already in the config — L-42 is the natural next step. |
| **L-43** | Job Application Process | Same rationale. FAFSA and job applications both live in the postsecondary pipeline. |
| **L-44** | Interview Skills & Workplace Readiness | Completes the career readiness sequence (L-41→L-42→L-43→L-44). (c)(15)(D) references quality of education investment including starting salary — interview skills connect directly. |
| **L-07** | Understanding Tax Brackets and Tax Rates | Strengthens (c)(3) compensation analysis — understanding how progressive taxation works is essential context for gross-to-net pay calculations. L-06 covers federal/state tax broadly; L-07 goes deeper on brackets. |

## Config 2: §113.76 — Personal Financial Literacy and Economics (SB 1063/2021)

**Course type:** Combined PFLE course (fulfills economics graduation credit)
**K&S statements:** 10 — (d)(1) through (d)(10)
**Unique to this course:** Economics standards (d)(1) and (d)(2), Entrepreneurship standard (d)(5)

### Reed's mapping produced 48 chapters. Adding L-71 = 49. To reach 45, removing 4:

| Removal | Title | Rationale |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| **L-58** | Supply, Demand & Market Structures | **Redundant.** L-50 (Supply and Demand) covers the core S&D content for (d)(1)(E-H). L-58 is an extended chapter that overlaps significantly. |
| **L-59** | Understanding Local Tax Structures | **Secondary.** Only supports (d)(2)(A) on progressive/regressive taxes at local level. L-06 and L-07 already cover federal/state tax structures adequately. |
| **L-52** | Government and the Economy | **Secondary.** Supports (d)(2)(E) macroeconomic goals, but L-53 (Fiscal/Monetary Policy) and L-54 (Inflation/Unemployment) cover the core macro content directly. |
| **L-51** | Market Structures and Consumer Choice | **Weakest link.** Only secondary support for (d)(5)(F) business organization types. L-45 (Entrepreneurship) covers the primary entrepreneurship content. |
